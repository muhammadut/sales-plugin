# Blount — Fanatical Prospecting

## One-line summary
Blount's book is the discipline-and-math case for prospecting every day, packaged with a 5-step phone framework and two operational rituals (Golden Hours, the 30-Day Rule) that prevent the pipeline collapse every solo founder eventually walks into.

## Why this tier
Scaffolding, not evidence. Blount is a career sales trainer who runs Sales Gravy; the book contains zero controlled studies and most of the numerical claims (e.g., "you need 30 dials to get one conversation") are aggregated trainer experience, not published research. What earns it scaffolding-tier is that the **ritual structure is a forcing function**: a founder who blocks Golden Hours and follows the 5-step phone framework will dial more and panic less than one who improvises. The 30-Day Rule in particular — "the prospecting you do today pays off 30 to 90 days from now" — is the anti-procrastination doctrine that solo founders need most. The framework predicts no outcome on its own; it only forces the work to happen.

## Key concepts
- **The 30-Day Rule.** Three weeks of inactivity will collapse your pipeline 30-90 days later, and the collapse is invisible until it hits.
- **Golden Hours.** Time-block the highest-leverage selling hours (typically Tuesday-Thursday morning and late afternoon) and protect them from email, internal meetings, and admin.
- **The 5-Step Phone Framework.** (1) Get their attention by name. (2) Identify yourself and your company. (3) State the reason for the call in one sentence. (4) Bridge with a "because" statement that ties to their world. (5) Ask for what you want — the meeting, the next step, the calendar slot.
- **Refusal to quit.** Most cold calls die at objection #1; persistence to objection #3 is where booked meetings happen.
- **The prospecting pyramid.** Leads, suspects, prospects, qualified opportunities, deals — keep volume at the top so attrition through the funnel does not starve the bottom.
- **Three Ps to avoid.** Procrastination, perfectionism, paralysis — the ways prospecting gets sabotaged by the prospector's own head.

Cited by `cadence-designer` when defending the daily-dial-volume target in the cadence (the elocal_clone math of 40-60 dials/day per caller traces back to this book's Golden Hours discipline). ## Anti-citation guidance
Do not cite Blount as evidence for any specific numerical claim (dials-to-conversations ratios, conversion rates, optimal call duration). The numbers are trainer aggregation, not published data — use Gong's 100k-call study for those. Do not let `script-writer` cite Blount and Rackham (SPIN) in the same script — they are different framework shapes and mixing them produces incoherent calls. Pick one per script.
