# HomeAdvisor / Angi Reverse Playbook

## One-line summary
Direct contractor-voice data from the largest North American home-services lead-gen platforms — distilled into a list of every concrete complaint pattern the elocal_clone script must explicitly disavow, because every contractor on a cold call has been burned by at least one of these and is listening for whether you sound like the company that burned them.

## Why this tier
Evidence, in the operational sense — the FTC enforcement action against HomeAdvisor (January 2023, $7.2M settlement) is a public legal record establishing that HomeAdvisor "made false, misleading, or unsubstantiated claims about the quality and source of the leads," confirming the contractor complaints are not just sour grapes. Beyond the FTC record, thousands of independent first-person contractor complaints across SmartCustomer (8,378+ reviews), Sitejabber (2,163+ reviews), BBB, PissedConsumer, and Reddit threads form a consistent pattern. **This is direct customer-voice data from the people the elocal_clone founder is selling to.** It is anecdotal at the per-contractor level but representative at the aggregate level: every plumber, electrician, and HVAC tech the founder calls has either personally been burned by HomeAdvisor or knows a peer who has. The reverse playbook is therefore a sharp competitive weapon — every script line that explicitly disavows a HomeAdvisor pattern earns trust the moment the contractor hears it.

## Key concepts (the patterns to disavow, ranked by complaint frequency)
- **Charging for bogus leads.** Contractors report being charged $30-80 per "lead" that turns out to be a wrong number, a disconnected line, a tire-kicker who never wanted service, or a homeowner who has been called by 5 other contractors in 10 minutes. Refund disputes are common and rarely succeed. **Reverse playbook:** "You don't pay until the homeowner answers the phone and says they want a quote. No charge for bad numbers, no charge for wrong category, no charge for tire-kickers."
- **Sending leads outside the contractor's stated category or geography.** Contractors set their service radius and category and still receive leads from the wrong town or wrong trade. The FTC complaint specifically cited this. **Reverse playbook:** "We honor your category and your service area exactly. If you're a plumber in Hamilton, you don't get electrician calls in Burlington."
- **Selling the same lead to 4-8 contractors simultaneously.** Contractors describe arriving at a quote appointment to find three other vans parked outside. **Reverse playbook:** "You get the call exclusively. We don't sell the same homeowner to anyone else."
- **Aggressive auto-renewal subscriptions.** Contractors report being unable to cancel, being auto-billed for unused months, and being threatened with collections after disputing. **Reverse playbook:** "Month-to-month, cancel any time, no contract, no auto-renewal trap."
- **Refund disputes that always go against the contractor.** Contractors universally describe submitting refund requests for bad leads and being denied without explanation. **Reverse playbook:** "If a call doesn't connect to a real homeowner with a real job in your category, we credit you automatically — no dispute process required."
- **Aggressive telemarketing of contractors.** Contractors are themselves cold-called repeatedly by HomeAdvisor sales reps after they sign up, often to upsell premium tiers. **Reverse playbook:** "We call you ONCE to onboard you, then we leave you alone. No upsell calls, no premium-tier pressure."
- **Reviews and reputation tied to platform.** Contractors lose access to their reviews if they leave the platform. **Reverse playbook:** "Your customers, your data — we don't lock you in."
- **Lead quality declining over time as the platform scales.** Older contractors describe the platform being good in year 1-2 and degrading as more contractors were added and lead quality dropped. **Reverse playbook:** "We cap contractors per category per region — we will not flood your zip code with competitors."
