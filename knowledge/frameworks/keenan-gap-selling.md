# Keenan — Gap Selling

## One-line summary
Keenan's reframing: stop selling features and benefits, start diagnosing the gap between the customer's current state and their desired future state — the bigger the gap, the bigger the deal, and the seller's only job is to make the gap visible and quantified.

## Why this tier
Scaffolding, not evidence. Keenan is a sales consultant (A Sales Growth Company) and the book is a practitioner manifesto, not a research study. There are no controlled trials, no large-n call analyses, no replicated findings. What earns it scaffolding-tier is the **discipline of writing down the current state and future state in concrete terms before pitching anything**. Most sellers skip this step and go straight to the demo or the pitch deck; Keenan forces the seller to enumerate the gap explicitly, which has the side effect of revealing whether there even IS a gap big enough to support a deal. For solo founders selling into trades businesses (where the buyer often does not articulate the problem in business terms), the current-state / future-state worksheet is a useful interview structure even if the broader framework is theoretical.

## Key concepts
- **Current State.** A complete description of where the customer is today: their workflows, costs, pain points, KPIs, emotional state about the situation. The seller's job is to know this in detail before pitching.
- **Future State.** A complete description of where the customer wants to be: their goals, the metrics that would change, the emotional state in the desired world. The customer often cannot articulate this without help.
- **The Gap.** The literal, quantified delta between Current State and Future State. The bigger the gap, the more the customer will pay to close it. No gap = no sale.
- **Four levels of pain.** (1) Physical / operational pain (broken processes), (2) financial pain (cost or lost revenue), (3) emotional pain (stress, fear, frustration), (4) impact pain (downstream effects on others). Successful sellers surface all four.
- **Problem-centric selling.** The product is irrelevant until the problem is fully diagnosed. The seller is a doctor, not a vending machine.
- **The PIC questions.** Problem (what is broken?), Impact (what does the breakage cost?), Cause (why is it happening?). A simpler interview spine than SPIN for non-technical buyers.

## When to cite
Cited by `script-writer` when the discovery section needs a problem-quantification step — the PIC questions (Problem, Impact, Cause) are the simplest discovery spine for trades buyers who do not speak in KPIs. Cited by `call-coach` when grading whether the user actually surfaced quantified pain or just sympathized with the prospect. Cited by `sales-researcher` as the framework for "we sell to a current-state-future-state gap, not to a feature list."

## Anti-citation guidance
Do not cite Gap Selling as a complete sales methodology — it is a discovery and qualification framework, not a closing or objection-handling system. Pair it with Voss for objection handling and with Blount for prospecting. Do not let `script-writer` use Gap Selling as the OPENER framework for cold calls; the gap diagnosis happens after the opener earns the right to ask.
