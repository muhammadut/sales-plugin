# Sobczak — Smart Calling

## One-line summary
Sobczak's argument: there is no such thing as a cold call once you have done the pre-call research — every "cold" call should be 5-10 minutes of upfront homework that lets you reference something specific about the prospect's business, which converts the call from interruption to relevance.

## Why this tier
Scaffolding, not evidence. Sobczak runs telephone-sales training (Business By Phone Inc.) and the book is a structured trainer's manual, not a research report. There are no controlled studies, no large-n call data. What earns it scaffolding-tier is the **pre-call research checklist**: it forces the seller to spend 5-10 minutes per prospect finding one specific thing — a recent press release, a Google review, a job posting, a social post — that grounds the opener in something real. The discipline beats the framework: a founder who does Sobczak's research checklist for every dial will outperform one who does not, regardless of script quality. The book is the antidote to "I have a list of 500 numbers and I'm just going to dial them all the same way."

## Key concepts
- **No such thing as a cold call.** Every call should be at least lukewarm by the time you pick up the phone — pre-call research is non-negotiable.
- **PVP — Possible Value Propositions.** Before the call, brainstorm 3-5 possible value propositions for THIS prospect specifically (not the generic pitch). Pick one to lead with based on what the research surfaced.
- **Smart Call Pre-Call Plan template.** A one-page worksheet per prospect: company name, contact name, role, recent news/event/trigger, possible value proposition, primary objective for the call, secondary objective if primary fails, anticipated objections, opening statement.
- **Opening statement structure.** (1) Greeting + name use, (2) reference to something specific (the research finding), (3) reason for the call tied to that reference, (4) the ask. The reference is what differentiates from generic openers.
- **Primary and secondary objectives.** Always have two objectives — the meeting (primary) and the information-gathering or referral (secondary). Never end a call with zero progress.
- **Voicemail as a touch.** Voicemails are a legitimate touch in a multi-touch sequence — short, specific, mention you'll send an email, mention a callback date.

## When to cite
Cited by `script-writer` when constructing the opener — the script template should include a `[INSERT RESEARCH FINDING]` slot that the user fills before each dial, per Sobczak's Smart Call Plan. Cited by `icp-analyst` when defending the practice of enriching the prospect list with one piece of per-record research before handing it to the dialer. Cited by `sales-researcher` whenever the founder asks "do I really need to research every single contact?" — answer is yes, and Sobczak is the citation.

## Anti-citation guidance
Do not cite Smart Calling as a high-volume sequence justification — Sobczak's model is research-heavy and lower-volume than Predictable Revenue's 150-250 emails/week. Pick one. Do not let the script-writer drop the `[INSERT RESEARCH FINDING]` slot from the template "for speed" — the slot is the entire point of citing Sobczak in the first place.
