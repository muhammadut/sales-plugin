# Weinberg — New Sales. Simplified.

## One-line summary
Weinberg's contribution is the **Sales Story** — a deliberately compressed power statement (roughly 15 words spoken, 30-60 seconds elaborated) that names the customer pain, the solution offered, and the proof, in language a non-sales human would actually use.

## Why this tier
Scaffolding, not evidence. Weinberg is a sales coach (New Sales Coach LLC) and the book is a structured trainer's manual targeted at small-business owners and sales managers. There are no studies. What earns it scaffolding-tier is the **Sales Story drafting exercise itself**: completing it forces the founder to commit to specific buyer pain language, specific differentiators, and specific proof points, all in 15 words. Most founders cannot complete this exercise on the first try and walk away with a clearer pitch than they had before. The exercise is the value; the framework around it is incidental.

## Key concepts
- **The Sales Story (Power Statement) structure.** (1) Customer pain or gain — the headline outcome the buyer cares about, (2) the solution your company offers in plain language, (3) one or two pieces of differentiation or proof. Compressed to ~15-30 words for the spoken opener; expandable to 60-90 seconds for the email or follow-up.
- **The Strategic Account List.** Pick 25-50 named target accounts. Pursue them deliberately. This is the small-business version of Predictable Revenue's named-account discipline.
- **Three Pillars of Sales Effectiveness.** (1) Targeted strategic account focus, (2) compelling sales story, (3) consistent prospecting effort. Skip any of the three and the system fails.
- **Stop pitching, start storytelling.** The Sales Story is structured around the **buyer's** pain, not the seller's product features. The product is the punchline, not the opening.
- **Eat your sales story.** Weinberg's discipline: every seller in the org should be able to recite the Sales Story from memory in their own words. If they can't, the org has no shared pitch.
- **The pre-call plan.** One sentence: "What is the desired outcome of this call?" If the seller can't answer that in a sentence, they're not ready to dial.

## When to cite
Cited by `script-writer` when constructing the value statement / power statement portion of the call — the Sales Story is the canonical structure for that 30-second segment. Cited by `sales-researcher` whenever the founder asks "what should my elevator pitch be?" — Weinberg's compression discipline is the answer. The Strategic Account List concept is also cited when the icp-analyst is debating list size with the founder.

## Anti-citation guidance
Do not cite Weinberg's Sales Story as a substitute for SPIN-style discovery — the Sales Story is a 30-second segment of the call, not the whole call. Do not let `script-writer` make the Sales Story the entire script; it is the value statement, nothing more. Weinberg's book is also written for B2B field sales with quotas and managers; a solo founder will need to mentally substitute "the founder" for every reference to "your sales rep."
