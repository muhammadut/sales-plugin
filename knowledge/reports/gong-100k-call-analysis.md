# Gong — 100k Cold-Call Analysis

## One-line summary
Gong's analysis of 100,000 recorded cold calls — and follow-up research across 300M total recorded sales calls — produced the most cited empirical findings in modern outbound sales: a 6.6x lift from the "How have you been?" opener, a 40% drop from the "Did I catch you at a bad time?" opener, a 55:45 talk-to-listen ratio for cold calls, and a 53-second average successful monologue length.

## Why this tier
Evidence, with appropriate vendor-source caveats. Gong is a conversation-intelligence platform whose business model depends on customers believing the platform produces actionable findings, so there is real source bias in what they choose to publish. **However**, the underlying methodology is the strongest in the public sales literature: actual recorded cold calls (not surveys, not self-reports) processed through the same speech-analysis pipeline, with outcomes labeled as "booked meeting" vs "no meeting" and patterns aggregated across the corpus. This is closer to a real observational field study than anything else in cold outbound. The n is genuinely 100k+ (and 300M for the broader corpus), the patterns have been replicated across multiple Gong publications and corroborated by independent observation in trained sales orgs, and Gong's findings have become the de facto standard taught in modern sales-development training. Treat as evidence-tier with the standing reminder that Gong's customer base is B2B SaaS sellers, not blue-collar trades cold calls — the patterns may transfer, may not, and the call-coach should label uncertain claims as "Gong-derived, untested in trades" when applied to elocal_clone.

## Key concepts
- **Talk-to-listen ratio: 55:45 for cold calls.** Successful cold calls have the SELLER talking 55% and the buyer 45%. This is the inverse of discovery calls (where listening more is better) — on cold calls, the buyer doesn't have the context to talk, so the seller carries the conversation. Top performers talk MORE on cold calls, not less.
- **Successful monologue length: 53 seconds; unsuccessful: 25 seconds.** When the seller does talk, they should hold the floor longer, not shorter. Successful sellers monologue (5+ uninterrupted seconds) at a rate of 20 monologues per call vs 12 for unsuccessful sellers. Average successful monologue lasts ~53 seconds; unsuccessful average ~25 seconds.
- **"How have you been?" opener: 6.6x lift.** Cold calls that open with "How have you been?" book meetings 6.6x more often than calls that don't. The mechanism is hypothesized to be familiarity priming (the prospect assumes you've spoken before and tries to remember).
- **"Did I catch you at a bad time?" opener: 40% drop.** This commonly taught "polite" opener REDUCES booked meetings by 40%. Counter-intuitive but replicated. The mechanism is hypothesized to be that the question primes the prospect to find a reason to say yes-it-is-a-bad-time.
- **Optimal call length: 5:50.** Successful cold calls average 5 minutes 50 seconds; unsuccessful ones cut off earlier. The seller should aim to keep the conversation going past the 5-minute mark.
- **Filler word density.** Successful cold calls have lower filler word density ("um," "uh," "like," "you know"). Filler density is a proxy for confidence and preparation.
- **Days and times.** Per Gong's published cold-call statistics, Wednesday and Thursday outperform Monday and Friday for connect rate; 4-5 PM local time and 10-11 AM local time outperform mid-morning and mid-afternoon.
- **State the reason for your call.** Successful sellers explicitly say "The reason I'm calling is..." which signals respect for the prospect's time and outperforms openers that meander into the pitch.

## Anti-citation guidance
Do not assume Gong's findings transfer cleanly to blue-collar trades sales — Gong's underlying corpus is B2B SaaS sellers calling B2B SaaS buyers. The 55:45 talk ratio and the 53-second monologue may behave differently when calling a plumber on his lunch break. The `call-coach` should mark Gong-derived claims as `[Gong B2B SaaS, transfer-untested]` when grading trades calls. Do not cite Gong for any sales motion that is NOT cold outbound calling — Gong has separate data for discovery calls, demos, etc., and the patterns reverse (e.g., discovery is 43:57 talk:listen, the opposite of cold).
