# Outreach + SalesLoft — Cadence Research

## One-line summary
The two largest sales-engagement platforms have published aggregate findings from hundreds of millions of touch attempts on their own platforms — converging roughly on 8-12 touches over 17-21 days, multi-channel, with the heaviest density in the first two weeks and a same-day call-plus-email opening touch in over 80% of top-performing cadences.

## Why this tier
Evidence, with a substantial caveat about source bias. Both Outreach and SalesLoft are sales-engagement vendors with a commercial interest in showing that sales-engagement platforms produce results, and the data they publish is filtered through that lens. **However**, the underlying datasets are real and the n is enormous: SalesLoft's published 2018 study analyzed ~200M sales interactions across 9000+ companies, and Outreach has published similar large-n findings on its 2024-2025 data. The convergence between the two independent datasets on the 8-12 touch, 17-21 day window is the strongest empirical signal available in cold-outbound cadence design. It is the closest thing the cold-outbound world has to Gong's cold-call data — vendor-published, but n large enough that the patterns are real.

## Key concepts
- **8-12 touches over 17-21 days.** The convergence point of multiple published Outreach and SalesLoft analyses for B2B prospect cadences. Below 6 touches and most prospects never reply; above 16 touches the marginal reply rate drops to near zero and you are wasting effort.
- **80%+ of top cadences open with a same-day call AND email on Day 1.** SalesLoft's 200M-interaction analysis: the highest-performing cadences fire the call and the email simultaneously on Day 1, not staggered.
- **Most replies arrive within the first 7 touches.** Outreach data: nearly all responses to a properly designed cadence happen by touch 7. Touches 8-12 catch the long tail. Touches beyond 12 are diminishing returns.
- **Multi-channel beats single-channel.** Cadences mixing call + email + LinkedIn outperform single-channel cadences (per Outreach's published analyses) by a meaningful margin, even when total touch count is held constant.
- **Front-load effort.** Top cadences put the heaviest touch density in the first two weeks. A cadence that spreads 12 touches evenly across 35 days underperforms a cadence that puts 8 touches in the first 14 days and 4 trailing touches in the next 21.
- **Personalization lifts open and reply rates.** Outreach: customized emails see ~10% higher open rates and ~2x higher reply rates vs templated emails. The marginal personalization cost is worth it to a point.

## When to cite
Cited by `cadence-designer` as the canonical evidence for the 8-12 touch, 17-21 day default cadence shape and for the Day-1 simultaneous call+email opening touch. Cited by `sales-researcher` when defending why the cadence should not stop at 3 touches (the founder's instinct) and should not stretch past 14 (overzealous instinct). ## Anti-citation guidance
Do not cite these vendors as if they were peer-reviewed research — they are platform-vendor publications with a clear commercial interest. Always note the source bias when citing. Do not assume the cadence shape transfers cleanly from B2B SaaS (their primary user base) to blue-collar trades outbound; the founder's vertical may have different rhythm. Refresh the blog URLs on every `/sales:research` run — vendor blogs are often updated and the headline numbers move.
