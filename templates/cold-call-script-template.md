## 10. Talk-track notes

Pacing, talk-to-listen target, words to use, words to avoid:

- **Talk-to-listen target:** 55:45 in favor of the prospect (Gong 100k-call analysis, `tier: evidence`).
- **Average monologue ceiling:** ~53 seconds before yielding to a question (Gong successful cold calls average 53s monologues, `tier: evidence`).
- **Pacing:** 150-170 words per minute. Faster than 180 wpm reads as nervous; slower than 130 wpm reads as condescending.
- **Words to use:** the prospect's name (3-5 times in the call), specific geography ("Hamilton"), specific vertical language ("RBQ license", "Mr. Rooter pricing", "after-hours work"), Phil M. Jones magic phrases ("Just out of curiosity…", "What makes you say that?"), Voss labels ("It sounds like…", "It seems like…").
- **Words to avoid:** "honestly" (signals dishonesty), "to be frank" (same), "obviously" (condescending), "guys" (use the prospect's name), "synergy"/"leverage"/"solutions" (B2B jargon), the names of the competitors as positive examples (only as contrast).
- **Filler word ceiling:** <5 fillers per minute ("um", "uh", "like", "you know"). Caller hears their own filler density in `/sales:coach` analysis.
